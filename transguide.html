Sefaria Transliteration Guidelines

General Guidelines:

  • Sefaria uses transliterations that reflect the modern Israeli pronunciation of Hebrew words.
  • Transliterated words should be italicized.
  • The definite article, when placed at the beginning of the word in book titles, is indicated by capitalizing both the article and the word itself (Ex: Sefer HaTerumah).
  • Prefixes are written as part of the word, without dashes or apostrophes (Ex: HaKetav Vehakabbalah).
  • No diacritical marks are used.
  • Dagesh chazak in Hebrew is indicated by a double consonant in English. (Ex: Hallel, Chanukkah).This is true unless it follows a prefix (Ex: HaTorah),or is inside a “ש” (in which case simply use “sh”).
  • The Hebrew letter “ח” is indicated by “ch”; the Hebrew letter כ is indicated by “kh.” (Ex: Challah (=bread), okhel (=eat)).
  • The Hebrew letter “ה” is indicated by “h,” no matter where it appears in the word. (Ex: Halakhah). However, there are exceptions for certain proper names (Ex: Moshe).
