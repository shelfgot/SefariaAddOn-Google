Support

What in the world is that button doing there? I offer you, dear user, the following explanation. There are many ways one could feasibly support this project. I originally built this tool to serve my purposes, and the options present reflect that. I couldn't have possibly predicted that at some point the add-on would be used upwards of 100,000 times a week. These days, much as I try to predict what will be most useful to the broadest spectrum of people, I know that there are uses that I cannot imagine. By sending me an email explaining how you use the add on and what features you would like to see, you enable me to update the add on and make using it a better experience for everyone! (And the encouragement I get from knowing that behind the usage statistics are real people using this add on is also a dose of motivation in and of itself!)

I am currently a college student with negative hours of free time a day, and it is easy to let this project languish in the dustbin of old projects - which in fact was the case for the time I spent in Yeshiva and most of my college career. When I receive positive feedback or actual investment in a certain project, I devote more of my time and my thought to it. As such, consider sending me an email at the address above or "buying me a coffee" (among other things! more to come!) at . All monetary contributions - after ma'aser kesafim (obligatory tithe) is taken - will be spent, bli neder, on development tools for the betterment of this add on and seforim (which will also benefit this add on, seeing as I use the add-on and think of ways to improve it when I learn Torah.) Something feels off about even coming close to soliciting donations in general, and especially when it comes to spreading Torah (but am heartened by the Yerushalmi's permissive stance on the topic).
